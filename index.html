    My name is Pavith Pramodya Dissanayake and I am from Sri Lanka. I graduated with a BSc in Physics, Mathematics, and Astronomy. All throughout my studies, I dove into core courses in physics, math, statistics, and geology, which gave me a solid foundation to understand how the world works.
    Astronomy has been a big fascination for me since I was a kid, and I was lucky enough to study it deeply for two years during my degree. I explored everything from spherical astronomy and celestial mechanics to observational techniques, which only made me more curious about the universe.
    For my final year project, I decided to step into the field of medical physics—and it turned out to be amazing! I found it truly fascinating to see how physics can be applied directly in medicine. This discovery ties in well with my work as a certified EMT and a regular community volunteer, where I provide emergency medical care and first aid. Maybe that’s why medical physics feels like such a natural fit for me.
    Here, you’ll mainly find my projects in astronomy and medical physics. I enjoy using machine learning and data analysis to explore new ideas and solve problems in these fields. Feel free to check out my projects—and I’d love to hear your thoughts or feedback!
